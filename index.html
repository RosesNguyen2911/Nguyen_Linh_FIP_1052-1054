About Algonquin Park

Algonquin Park, Ontario’s first provincial park, established in 1893, is a haven of untouched wilderness. Spanning 7,630 km², it features pristine lakes, rugged rock formations, and diverse wildlife like moose and wolves. This world-renowned sanctuary offers a glimpse into a vanishing natural past. Customer Experience

Just 15 minutes from Bear Trail Couples Resort, the $10 million Visitor Centre is a must-visit destination. Built in 1993 to celebrate Algonquin Park’s centennial, it features world-class exhibits showcasing the park's rich natural and human history. Algonquin Park, rich in history and culture, has inspired books, films, scientific works, and the art of Tom Thomson and the Group of Seven. It offers visitors a stunning natural setting and diverse recreational activities.

Algonquin Galeairy Lake More Details

Couples Resort sits on the shores of Galeairy Lake, bordering Algonquin Park. Once called Long Lake, it was renamed for its location between Nightengale and Airy townships.

Galeairy Lake, 11 km long in Algonquin Park, is a peaceful spot for paddling, wildlife viewing, and enjoying picnics or swims along its tranquil shores.
